1. Nouns

A noun names a person, place, thing, or idea.

  • 1. Aayush is a student.
  • 2. She bought a car.
  • 3. The dog barked loudly.
  • 4. Love is a strong feeling.
  • 5. Varanasi is a city.
  • 6. Books are kept in the library.
  • 7. His happiness was visible.
  • 8. Teachers guide students.
2. Pronouns

Pronouns replace nouns to avoid repetition.

  • 1. He is my brother.
  • 2. She loves reading.
  • 3. It is raining.
  • 4. They are playing football.
  • 5. We are going to school.
  • 6. Give it to him.
  • 7. This is mine.
3. Verbs & Tenses

Verbs show actions or states. Tenses show time.

  • 1. I eat breakfast every day. (Present)
  • 2. She went to the market. (Past)
  • 3. They will travel tomorrow. (Future)
  • 4. He is running now. (Present Continuous)
  • 5. We have finished our homework. (Present Perfect)
  • 6. She had left before I arrived. (Past Perfect)
  • 7. They will have completed it by Monday. (Future Perfect)
4. Adjectives & Adverbs

Adjectives describe nouns. Adverbs describe verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs.

  • 1. She is a beautiful girl. (Adjective)
  • 2. He runs quickly. (Adverb)
  • 3. The movie was very interesting. (Adjective + Adverb)
  • 4. They arrived early.
  • 5. She spoke softly.
  • 6. It is a huge building.
  • 7. He solved the problem efficiently.
5. Prepositions & Conjunctions

Prepositions show relationships. Conjunctions connect words or clauses.

  • 1. The cat is on the roof.
  • 2. I am going to school.
  • 3. He came after lunch.
  • 4. I like tea and coffee.
  • 5. She is smart but lazy.
  • 6. You can stay here or come with me.
  • 7. I stayed because I was tired.
6. Articles & Subject-Verb Agreement

Articles (a, an, the) specify nouns. Subject-Verb Agreement ensures subject and verb match in number.

  • 1. I saw a dog.
  • 2. She is an engineer.
  • 3. The sun rises in the east.
  • 4. He runs fast.
  • 5. They are students.
  • 6. My brother likes football.
  • 7. The dogs bark loudly.
